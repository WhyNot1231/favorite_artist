5 Most Popular Works


5. Animals, 1977

Animals, Pink Floyd's 10th album overall, was a concept album loosely based on George Orwell's Animal Farm. There were no singles released from it, since the only songs that weren't a minute-and-a-half long were over 10 minutes long.

4. The Division Bell, 1994

Though recorded and written with only two of Pink Floyd's founding members, the 1994 release, The Division Bell, was still able to reach double platinum within the year it was released. It received multiple mixed reviews, but is still one of the most popular Pink Floyd albums.

3. The Wall, 1979

The Wall was Pink Floyd's concept album that came directly after Animals, and was almost entirely written and conceptualized by lead singer and bassist, Roger Waters. It's reception, like others, was mixed, however it did extremely well in numer of albums-sold, and its lead single, "Another Brick in the Wall Part 2," reached number 1 on the Billboard chart in 6 countries, including the US and UK. The Wall was certified 23 times platinum 20 years after its release, a monumental achievement.

2. Wish You Were Here, 1975

Given their success from their previous album, Wish You Were Here was a hard follow-up album. It was both a loose concept-album and a part-tribute to former and founding member Syd Barrett, who suffered from schizophrenia among other things, and gradually starting to become more and more absent.

1. The Dark Side of the Moon, 1973

The Dark Side of the Moon is by far Pink Floyd's most popular album, and it received all the critical acclaim it deserved. Like others, it's also a concept album, which explores many psychological things, including mental illness, inspired by previously-mentioned former band member Syd Barrett. DSOTM is one of the best-selling albums ever, and even though it was only in the first place on the Billboard 200 for a week, it remained on the chart from 1973 to 1988, totaling 741 weeks.
